Final Prototype Design And Implementation

Motivation

College students have been disregarding their emotional and physical health due to unrealistic productivity expectations set by themselves and others. In response to it, our group decided to create a tangible stress ball which the will help the users relax and destress through meditation. The user can squeeze the stress ball and recieve feedback and the device can also be used as guide to a meditation practice. Medidate Me, the tangible stess ball is small and portable which allow the user to use at anytime and any location.

Why Meditate-Me!!!

Meditate-Me combines two forms of stress relief for a successful meditation in just five minutes!
First, for one minute, the user can squeeze the Meditate-Me and receive feedback vibrations.
Then, for four minutes, the user can follow the light and vibration guides to complete a box breathing exercise: breathe in for four seconds, hold for four seconds, release for four seconds.








Meditate Me is Tangible User Interface which guides the user through a five minute meditation and relieves stress.

Design and Implementation

For this interface we went through three design iterations. First, we drew some initial sketches of what we wanted our interface to look like. Next, we created a prototype that was high-fidelity in look but did not function mechanically. Our second prototype was medium fidelity in look and function. And finally our last prototype was high-fidelity in look and function.

Initial Design

Here are some drawings we made of our initial design showing the various elements we would need for the system to function

First Prototype

Our first prototype was designed to be high-fidelity in look and feel but not functional mechanically. This prototype was hand-sewn with thread and fabric, and filled with stuffing and quarters for weight.


Second Prototype Mechanics

Our second prototype was medium fidelity in function. This model completed the box breathing part of the meditation exercise. Using an Arduino Uno and two motors, the system vibrated on/off for four seconds each.

Second Prototype Look

Our second prototype was medium fidelity in look. This model was hot glued, and used velcro to open and close. It was made with fabric and plastic and filled with stuffing.

What is inside it

Meditate-Me is made of an Ardunio Uno, an Adafruit Flora, a Neopixel Ring, two vibration motors, a flex sensor, and many many wires! The outside fabric is hand sewn with a soft fabric and closes with a zipper.

Here is the inside of the Meditate-Me. The Neopixel light ring is attached to the front of the fabric with a detachable velcro strap. The flex sensor is velcroed into the bottom of the fabric as well, so it is always easy for the user to bend.

Challenges We Faced

We overcame many challenges to complete this project

Since this was our first time using mechanical wiring and Arduino/Adafruit products, we overcame many challenges making Meditate-Me.
Mainly we struggled with making our prototype smaller, and more durable. To overcome this, we soldered all of our circuits. This made our interface stronger and more compact.
Main Challenges:

  • Fragile materials
  • Flex sensor difficulty
  • Needs two computers to run
